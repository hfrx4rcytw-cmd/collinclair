2023-12-28

On sketching and seeing

I've been carrying a small notebook everywhere. Quick drawings of whatever catches my eye—a corner of a room, the way light hits a table, someone's posture on the train.

[ SKETCH STUDIES ]

These sketches aren't meant to become anything. They're just practice in looking. The act of drawing forces you to actually see what's in front of you, not what you think you see.

"Drawing is not what one sees, but what one can make others see." —Edgar Degas

Most of these will stay in the notebook. But sometimes a gesture or composition will stick with me and find its way into a painting weeks later.

Photos from the coast

Spent a few days walking along the shore last week. Brought my camera but mostly just watched the water. There's something hypnotic about the repetition—waves coming in, going out, endlessly.

[ COASTAL PHOTOGRAPH 1 ]

I shot on film, which means I won't see these images for another week or two. I like that delay. It separates the act of seeing from the act of looking at what you saw. Forces you to trust your instincts in the moment.

[ COASTAL PHOTOGRAPH 2 ]

Planning to use some of these as reference for a new painting series. Or maybe they'll just exist as photographs. We'll see.

2023-11-30

Why I make things

Someone asked me recently why I work across so many different mediums—painting, photography, music, writing. The honest answer is that I don't really think of them as separate.

They're all just different ways of paying attention. Sometimes an idea wants to be a painting. Sometimes it's a photograph. Sometimes it's three chords and a feeling. The medium doesn't matter as much as the impulse to make something that wasn't there before.

I think calling myself a "painter" or "photographer" would be limiting. I'm just someone who makes things. The label matters less than the practice.

This multidisciplinary approach also keeps things fresh. When I'm stuck on a painting, I'll pick up my camera. When I'm not sure what to photograph, I'll write. They feed each other.
